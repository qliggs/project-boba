# Product Decisions

## Cozy emotional goals

Project Boba should feel:

- warm
- calm
- soft
- safe
- supportive
- proud in a gentle way

It should not feel cold, harsh, loud, or stressful.

## No punishment philosophy

This product is not built around shame.

That means:

- no punishment mechanics
- no guilt-heavy language
- no harsh loss states
- no "you failed" energy

The app should encourage, not pressure.

## Avatar-first design

The avatar is the emotional center of the app.

The home screen should feel like opening a little cozy companion world.

That means:

- the avatar stays central
- the background matters
- customization matters
- catchphrases matter

## Shop philosophy

The shop should be real, but small and simple in v0.

It should reward progress with:

- cosmetics
- backgrounds
- small personalization
- phrase packs
- gentle delight

The shop is for cozy reward and expression, not aggressive monetized-style pressure.

## Streak philosophy

Streaks should encourage, not punish.

Current rule:

- 3 completed tasks qualifies a day

If a day is missed:

- do not make the app feel mean
- do not add shame
- do not make the user feel scolded

## Tag and reward-track philosophy

Tags should help connect task types to reward flavors.

Examples:

- Hygiene -> clean, spa, soft cozy unlocks
- Chores -> homey, cabin, practical cozy unlocks
- Creative -> whimsical, colorful, playful unlocks

The system should stay simple in v0, but expandable later.

## Android-first, Mac-preview-supported reasoning

Android is the real product target.

Why:

- it is the actual platform we want to ship on
- it matches the original product goal
- it is the real production path

Mac preview support exists because:

- it is faster for local product validation on your Mac
- it gives a quick way to test the feel of the app
- it helps shape UX without blocking on Android-only setup

The Mac app is a support tool.

The Android app remains the main product.
